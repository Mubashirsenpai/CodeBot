History

The park's lands were set aside as a wildlife refuge in 1958.
In 1971, the small human population of the area was relocated and the lands were designated to a national park.
The park has not seen major development as a tourist location since its original designation.
The Mole National park as a protective area is underfunded and national and
international concerns exist about poaching and sustainability in the park,
but its protection of important resident antelope species has improved since its initial founding as a preserve.
Since the resettlement of humans from the area,
the park has become an important study area for scientists.
This has allowed for some long-term studies, in particular,
of relatively undisturbed sites compared to similar areas of densely populated equatorial West Africa.
One study on the resident population of 800 elephants,
for example, indicates that elephant damage to large trees varies with species.
